# PricingNest Approval Baseline

**Date:** 2026-07-02

## Scope

Reviewed the approval-critical and trust-critical pages currently visible on the site:

- `/about/`
- `/contact/`
- `/privacy-policy/`
- `/cookie-policy/`
- `/terms/`

## Baseline Findings

### Stronger areas

- The site already has a visible About page with editorial responsibility and review language.
- The Contact page already supports correction requests and operational feedback.
- The site has a privacy policy, a cookie policy, and a terms page.
- Trust signals are already visible in the layout and in the stronger retained pages.

### Weak areas

- `Terms` was too thin and read more like a placeholder disclaimer than a real operating page.
- `Privacy` and `Cookie` were present but still short for a site that wants stable AdSense approval.
- The site has a lot of short guide and glossary pages, which increases the risk that the property is judged as thin even if many pages are already noindexed.
- The current AdSense readiness path still needs explicit verification in a dedicated checklist.

### Likely approval risks

- site looks content-heavy but not yet deeply differentiated enough
- policy pages may appear too lightweight during review
- low-value support pages may still create a thin-site impression
- AdSense readiness may be incomplete if code and policy rollout are not aligned

## Recommended Pre-Application Fixes

1. Expand Terms into a real operating document.
2. Expand Privacy and Cookie policies with clearer data, consent, and retention language.
3. Keep strengthening About and Contact with real editorial and correction workflow details.
4. Refresh the top calculator pages so they communicate a clearer decision promise.
5. Re-check the ad and consent path before submitting again.

## Decision

Do not treat the current site as fully ready for another AdSense submission until the policy pages and click-capture pages have been updated and rechecked.
