# Retained Page Enhancement Wave 2 Design

## Goal

Strengthen the next set of thin but still-indexable pages so the retained search surface continues to look authored,
bounded, and decision-oriented instead of inventory-like.

## Scope

This wave stays intentionally narrow and only upgrades six retained pages:

- Guides
  - `nrr-playbook`
  - `ltv-cac-benchmarks`
- Glossary
  - `feature-gating`
  - `egress`
  - `cac-payback`
  - `time-to-value`

## Why These Pages

These pages still sit on the retained indexable surface, but they read too much like short checklist entries or generic
metric definitions. They are exactly the kind of pages that can make the site feel broader than it is strong.

They are worth keeping only if they act like real editorial entry points into pricing decisions, retention diagnosis,
and unit-economics interpretation.

## Design Principles

- Add judgment, not filler.
- Keep each page focused on one concrete commercial question.
- Show where the concept is useful and where teams misuse it.
- Tie the page into core calculators and deeper retained guides so the site feels like a system.
- Do not expand scope into new pages, new templates, or broad governance changes.

## Upgrade Pattern

### Guides

For `nrr-playbook` and `ltv-cac-benchmarks`, replace short checklist framing with:

- when the metric or ratio becomes strategically important
- the core interpretation patterns that matter in pricing work
- the most common analytical mistakes
- a next-step bridge into calculators or stronger retained pages

### Glossary

For `feature-gating`, `egress`, `cac-payback`, and `time-to-value`, move beyond definition-plus-checklist and add:

- why the concept matters in pricing or growth decisions
- where the model breaks or is commonly overread
- how the concept links to related pricing tools, guides, or adjacent metrics

## Success Criteria

- The six pages no longer read like thin inventory content.
- Each page contains at least one interpretation layer and one limitation or misuse layer.
- Internal links support real navigation toward pricing decisions, not generic cross-linking.
- Site governance, audit, and build health remain clean after the edits.
