## What this page is for

Annual renewal strategy is the operating plan for handling annual customers before term end. It covers timing,
segmentation, messaging, value review, and how to avoid treating renewals like brand-new deals.

The page is useful when the team needs a repeatable renewal motion, not just a reminder to "contact customers before
renewal."

## What to confirm before renewal season

Before renewals start, confirm:

- which cohorts are highest risk
- whether customers have seen enough value to justify the current price
- whether support or usage has changed materially since the last term
- whether upgrade or downgrade paths are clear
- whether the discount policy still matches the business's pricing anchor

If those inputs are unresolved, the team will end up improvising at the exact moment it should be most consistent.

## Why it matters

Annual renewals hide a lot of risk if the business only looks at booked revenue. A customer can renew and still be a
weak account if value realization is poor, usage is drifting down, or the discount logic is resetting the market price
anchor.

The right renewal strategy protects retention without making the business train customers to wait for concessions.

## How to handle the renewal motion

Segment renewals by size and risk, then review usage, support, and ROI before sending the renewal proposal. If value is
clear, keep the process simple. If value is not clear, the issue may be packaging or onboarding rather than the renewal
itself.

Send communication early enough that the customer has time to ask questions before the term ends. Late outreach often
creates unnecessary discount pressure and downgrade risk.

## Common mistakes

- Treating renewals as if they were new logo sales.
- Ignoring usage trends and value delivery before the renewal email goes out.
- Mixing renewal churn with logo churn in the same analysis.
- Using discounts to solve unclear value perception.
