## What this page is for

Churn survey insights turn cancellation feedback into product, pricing, or onboarding decisions. This page is useful
when the team has survey data but still needs to decide what it actually means.

The main point is not to collect more comments. It is to convert churn reasons into changes the business can test.

## What to ask

Keep the survey short and specific. Ask about:

- price
- value delivered
- missing features
- onboarding friction
- support experience
- plan fit

That gives the team enough structure to detect patterns without making the survey too hard to complete.

## Why it matters

Raw churn feedback is often messy. People describe value gaps as price objections, and they describe pricing mistakes as
product complaints. The survey only becomes useful when the team codes responses consistently and connects them to a
real action path.

This is especially important when churn comments are concentrated in one plan or customer segment. The issue may be
packaging, onboarding, or renewal timing rather than generic dissatisfaction.

## How to turn survey data into action

Group answers by plan and cohort, then separate price objections from product objections. If the same issue repeats,
look for the page or process that should absorb the fix:

- packaging or tier fit
- onboarding or time to value
- renewal handling
- support burden
- pricing page clarity

If the survey only produces anecdotes, the business is not yet using the data as a decision tool.

## Common mistakes

- Asking too many questions.
- Ignoring small-plan feedback because the revenue is lower.
- Treating every churn reason as a price problem.
- Leaving free-form answers uncoded and unsearchable.
